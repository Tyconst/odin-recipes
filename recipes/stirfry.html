Steak and Broccoli Stir Fry

A bowl of steak and broccoli with rice.

A classic steak and broccoli stir-fry features tender, thinly sliced beef and vibrant, crisp-tender broccoli florets, stir-fried quickly in a hot pan or wok. The steak is typically cut against the grain to ensure tenderness, often marinated in a combination of soy sauce, cornstarch, and other seasonings to enhance flavor and promote a silky texture.

The broccoli is usually cooked to a crisp-tender texture, often achieved through quick stir-frying, steaming, or blanching, retaining its vibrant green color and maximizing nutrient retention.

The dish is brought together by a savory and flavorful stir-fry sauce. This sauce often includes ingredients like soy sauce, oyster sauce (for umami depth), beef broth, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, and a touch of sweetness from ingredients like honey or brown sugar. A cornstarch slurry is often added to thicken the sauce, creating a glossy coating for the beef and broccoli.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoon grapeseed oil or other cooking oil
  • 1 pound ribeye steak, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 cups soy sauce, or more as needed
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh ginger, chopped
  • 1 shallot, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh broccoli florets
  • 2 green onions, chopped, for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat grapeseed oil in a cast iron skillet over medium heat. Season steak with salt and pepper.
  2. Add steak to skillet and cook for 1 to 2 minutes on each side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Whisk soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and shallot together in a bowl. Pour sauce into the skillet and bring to a gentle simmer. Add broccoli and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Add steak back into the skillet; cook until hot, 1 to 2 minutes. Sprinkle with chopped green onions to serve.
